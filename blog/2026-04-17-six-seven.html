2026-04-17 Six Seven

While I’m at it, let me talk about how fascinating and deeply disturbing the 6-7 mania is as an example of pure, unadulterated herd behavior.

Typically, herd behavior involves actions that are, on their own, meaningful in some way; people may vote for the same candidate, listen to the same songs, wear the same clothes, chant the same political slogans, but those votes, songs, clothes, and slogans all have cultural significance on their own.

But when people, in a frenzy of mindless enthusiasm, scream “6-7” and move their arms up and down opposite each other, the phrase and the gesture have literally no meaning on their own. Yet they engage on that behavior only because everyone else is. They partake of the mob’s emotional charge without borrowing any meaningful emotion from the mob–such as the courage to charge into battle, or the righteous anger of a political movement, or the pride for a sporting team’s achievement–nothing except an automatic and absurd rush, devoid of sense and significance. It’s not even obscene; it’s just what it is.

It’s a display of pure, automatic mimickry that you’d expect in primates other than homo sapiens. But there it is. Leave it to the Internet to unlock our base nature. It has isolated a fundamental psychological mechanism that makes social conformity possible in the same way that chemists isolate compounds responsible for a certain reaction.

And all it took was one Tik Tok video of a boy at a basketball game.
